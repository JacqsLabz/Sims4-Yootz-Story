Like at some point a cure is going to become available, but by that time things have shifted. She's not going to want the cure, like at first you know "oh we found the cure" and/or "we can finally cure this" but she's like I need time to think about this. Because what she feels isn't unified, so she needs time to sort through her feelings and figure them out. 

And she does and she takes some time. She really takes a good look at herself and really thinks about it and really gets in touch with the more subconscious parts of herself, not just the wolf, there's plenty of parts of the brain that are nonverbal, and doesn't want the cure. 

She doesn't want to remove the wolf, the wolf taught her how to take care of herself. I mean yeah the wolf did alter to some degree what taking care of herself means (being a werewolf fundamentally altered her biology), but it also taught her to make herself and her needs a priority in her life. In addition, it's not just a dumb animal, it has a certain wisdom to it. It taught her how there's a such thing as thinking too much (I think therefore I am confused), how being to clever can prevent understanding (how the conscious mind and one's preconceived ideas is/are the problem at times). 

Also the wolf isn't something separate, it is a piece of her. So she's really just getting in touch with herself on a profoundly deep level. I think that's ultimately going to be why she doesn't want a cure and she's fine the way she is. Sure she has the instincts of a wolf, but most of that is superficial and she doesn't mind them. So what if she eats raw meat sometimes? Her mother drinks blood and can't even eat food. One of her grandmothers went from a steady diet of bloodfruit to emotions. Her aunt like to flirt with the mortal veil, and does a "going ghost" type of thing on a regular basis. Oh yeah and her cousin is currently living on another planet. Everyone has... quirks. 

I guess I'm saying that yes it some ways it did alter and change her, but things like being able to smell more aren't deep changes. They aren't core parts of who she really is inside. And the changes to her core that came from the wolf are changes that were for the better. She doesn't want to lose the source of that wisdom. Plus she's kind of attached to that part of herself, and again she sees it as fine just the way it is.
