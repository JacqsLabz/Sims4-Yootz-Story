![](../assets/img/03.02/06-20-18_12-22-30%20AM.png)

Emma made herself a bloody mary, then joined the others at the table as Sara was telling a story. 

![[../assets/img/03.02/06-20-18_12-22-16 AM.png]]

When Sara finished, Jane smiled at Emma, "I made plenty of salad and put it in the fridge. Feel free to grab yourself a bowl or two sweetie."

"I think just stick to this for now," Emma held her cup, "But thanks mom."

"She hasn't been feeling well lately." Anne started. 

![](../assets/img/03.02/06-20-18_12-20-31%20AM.png)

Sara shifted in her chair, "What's wrong dear?" 

![](../assets/img/03.02/06-20-18_12-23-06%20AM.png)

Jane got up to clean the dishes and Emma held her belly, "I'm not sure; here recently I've felt so strange, especially my stomach. I don't know, I guess my gut has been hurting and upset off and on since the aliens."

Sara listened intently. "How about we see what I can feel with my power, ok?" 

Emma nodded, "Yes, I'd like that." It would be nice to have a better understand of how her sort-of-ghost, sort-of-vampire weirdness was interacting with whatever the aliens had done. It would be nice to know what was going on. And her mom could always feel these things. 

So they stood at the end of the table. 

![](../assets/img/03.02/06-20-18_12-32-27%20AM.png)

Sara took a moment to really focus on her dark magic, then she felt another magic that she hadn't felt in a while: alien magic. But it was concentrated in... Emma's womb? She shifted her magic to get a better feel, and couldn't believe what she felt: it was small, but it was fully alien, just like the ones that had abducted Sara for a brief time. "Emma...." Sara tried to start, but she lost the words before she could say them. 

Emma hesitated, but Sara didn't say more. "What is it Mom? How bad is it?" Emma felt a twinge of fear at hearing the tone Sara used. Was she going to be a ghost again?

"No, it's not bad." It just wasn't supposed to be possible. Sara shook herself. No, clearly it was because she felt it. Sara took a deep breath to center herself before trying again. "Honey, you're pregnant." 

Emma froze and her eyes went big. "I'm what?" 

"Let's get you sitting back down again, ok?" Sara help guide Emma to her chair again, before sitting her own. 

Anne's face was covered in surprise, "So we're pregnant at the same time? Emma is going to have a child too? What are the odds of that?"

![](../assets/img/03.02/06-20-18_12-26-17%20AM.png)

The word 'child' echoed in Emma's head, until it turned into an otherworldly tone. "So that's what the grim reaper meant..." Emma put her hand to forehead. "Oh gosh." 

Emma wasn't sure if Sara or Anne said anything else, everything became a haze as she felt lightheaded and even more out of sorts than before. 

"I think I need to lay down." Emma said. She tried to stand but found herself unsteady on her feet. 

Sara was around the table in an instant. "Careful sweetie." Sara held her up. "Let me help you, ok?"

Emma bit her lip and nodded, "Ok Mom." Her voice was soft and sad. 

Sara picked up Emma, "Do you want the couch or your coffin downstairs?"

"I'd rather a bed..." Emma whispered. 

Sara nodded then carried Emma upstairs to one of the beds they weren't using, and helped her get under the covers. 

When Sara came back downstairs, Jane looked worried. "Will she be ok?"

"Physically she's ok. She's a bit weak and needs to drink more, but she's ok, at least for now." Sara came over and sat at the bar, "Emotionally? I'd say she probably just needs some time?" Sara sighed, "It's a lot to take in at once." Her eyes went distant, as she thought about the memories. 

Jane nodded, "I imagine so." She remembered what it been like for Sara, so long ago. Her eyes wondered to the TV over above the fireplace. Emma had always loved those popcorn movie nights. She start looking through the cabinets. She was pretty sure- yes there is was. Jane put the popcorn machine on the counter. 

That got Sara's attention. "What's up?" 

"I'm going to try making Emma some popcorn. Well, here in a little bit. But first, I need to make sure I have everything to make it the same way as always. This might require a trip to the store." 

Both turned to the door of the downstairs bedroom as they heard Cass wake up. They turned back to each other, then to Anne. 

"I'll get her." Anne stood, "So you can focus on Emma." 

----

After a while, Jane headed upstairs with a bowl of popcorn and a mug of warm chamomile tea. 

She knocked softly on the door, "It's me Emma sweetie." She pushed open the door, came over next to the bed, and set the bowl and mug on the nightstand.
