Emma is sitting resting after a long day of her day job then helping Sara with the rocket. Ideally with a blanket, maybe in the backyard. 

"You know, you don't have to push yourself so hard." Sara came outside carrying two drinks, and offered one to Emma. 

"Thanks mom." Emma's tired face smiled. "I'll be ok." 

"You're human now, stop trying to keep up with me." Sara gave her a serious look. 

"I'm not... Ok, I guess maybe I am. But I don't want to leave all the work to you. Your job is so demanding, and this rocket project isn't easy."

"My job is a cakewalk for me, and I can handle building a rocket. I don't need sleep, you do." 

Emma waved her hand, "I told you, I'm fine."

"Have you seen the bags under your eyes?" Sara paused. "I really appreciate that you want to help me dear, but you can't be there for Brenda properly when you're this dead tired."

Maybe Sara is worried about her daughter. She knows what it is to want to be there for a child, but she is worried that Emma is going a bit far. Everything Emma does is for Brenda, and Emma at least for a time doesn't do anything for herself.
